    FUNPEDIA


    Planning ahead can be hard! People are working more than ever, and their free time is precious. How can we help people reclaim their leisure time, with a mobile app solution?


    We provide users with an quick and personal research method, that caters to last-minute plans, allowing them to spend less time thinking about fun things, and more time doing them!


    Let’s help users find more time, for fun time!


    Platform

    Mobile App Design

    Timeframe

    16 Days + 2 days of individual development

    Team

    Greg Ng, (Student) UX Designer Nanako Kawaharada, (Student) UX Designer Shelaam Janmohammed, (Student) UX Designer Veronika Latawiec, (Student) Project Manager/Designer

    My Role

    I was responsible for project management, as well as storyboarding, app branding, wireframing and prototyping. All updates to the app past mid-fidelity were solo additions in order to bring the prototype into high-fidelity.

    Project Outcomes

    80% of users said they would use this application to help them plan

    90% of users were able to effectively personalize their reccommendations.

    80% of users were able to confidently share activities.

    Ideation


    Next, we had to decide how to best meet the needs of people like Craig.


    First, we brainstormed things we liked about our app, wished the app could do, and what if we could develop it further. We then voted to advance ideas, and prioritized them in a feature prioritization matrix. We then kept both high impact, and low complexity ideas and voted forward one more feature.

    Key App Features


    In order to provide a user based experience, our app had to be quick, personalized and facilitate group planning.

    Therefore, the final key features that we prioritized were


  • Instant reccommendation refresh button
  • Preference options, and user questionnaire
  • Quick and intuitive sharing functionality

    Testing

    Outcomes


  • 100% of users were able to create an account
  • 90% of users were able to find and complete the user questionnaire
  • 40% of users were able to efficiently refresh a recommendation
  • 80% of users were able to share a recommendation

    • Overall, users had the most difficulty with understanding the app purpose, and refreshing the app recommendations.

    Iterations


  • Improved prototype flow (simplified transitions, added typing functionality etc.)
  • Edited confusing elements (added arrow to filters, rather than implied scroll)
  • Added refresh button text and colour to improve clarity and signify importance
  • Added colour to important elements

    • Then, during individual development, I added high fidelity components and design in order to create a finalized prototype that reflected our project goals.


    Reflection

    Takeaways


    Users can use our application to reduce their stress and time spent when researching and choosing activities, especially for groups of people.

    We worked to provide a way to incorporate a little spontaneity and fun into day-to-day life, and we have developed a solid foundation that can effectively be developedin the future.

    Future Development
